The build · in plain words

Your PC build — explained simply

The whole plan in easy, everyday language — so it's simple to read and simple to explain to the person building it. (The detailed technical version with exact sizes is linked at the bottom.)

Blue = cool air coming in. Red = hot air going out. The front of the case is on the left.

The big idea in one line: cool air comes in low, travels up through the powerful card, and hot air leaves at the top. Nothing is squeezed — every part has its own spot.

What's going inside

  • A very powerful main card for the AI work — this is the "RTX PRO 6000". It's the important one.
  • A second graphics card — the "RTX 5090". It is water-cooled: it comes with a separate cooling part (like a small car radiator) joined to it by two soft pipes.
  • The brain of the PC (the processor / "CPU") — it also has a water-cooling radiator, joined by pipes.
  • The power box that runs everything (the "power supply").
  • All of it inside one very large case — the "Lian Li V3000 Plus".

⭐ The one thing to get right — please tell the builder

The big AI card breathes air from underneath itself. So under that card we put only fans, to blow cool air up into it. We must never put a radiator under this card — a radiator there would feed it warm air and make it run hot and slow. Radiators go on the front and the top; fans go under the big card.

Where each part goes

The big AI card (RTX PRO 6000)In the lower slot — sits low, just above the cool-air fans.
The second card (RTX 5090)In the upper slot, just above the big card.
The 5090's radiatorOn the front of the case.
The CPU's radiatorOn the top of the case — it blows hot air out the top.
FansOn the floor and on the shelf just under the big card — they push cool air up.
The power boxAt the bottom, in its own compartment.
Cables + the small storage drivesHidden on the back side of the case, out of the way.

How the air moves

  • Cool air comes in at the bottom (through a dust filter) and the front.
  • It rises up through the big card.
  • Hot air goes out the top.
  • So: cool in low, hot out high — the two never mix. That's the whole idea.

How it sits under the desk

  • The PC stands on a small stand with wheels, so you can roll it out easily to dust it.
  • The desk's lowest height is locked, so the desk can never come down and press on top of the PC. There's always a gap above it.
