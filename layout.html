Reading the diagram

The one rule that drives everything: the PRO 6000 breathes from below and vents up. So the bottom of the case is fresh-air fans only, and the heat is pushed out the top.

IN The cool side (bottom & front)

  • Floor fans + dust filter — pull the coolest, cleanest air in the room up through the filtered floor. The pedestal lifts this off the ground so it breathes freely.
  • Above-PSU shelf fans — sit directly under the 6000 and lift that fresh air straight up into the card's underside. This is the money shot for a flow-through GPU.
  • Front radiator = the 5090 Astral LC's rad — its thick 65 mm body fits easily in the deep front, and the 5090's coolant gets fresh outside air (its best temps).

OUT The hot side (top & rear)

  • The 6000 vents upward into the case — that 600 W has to leave.
  • Top radiator = the DeepCool LT720 (CPU), running as exhaust — it sits right above the 6000 and carries its rising heat straight out the top.
  • Rear fan clears the pocket behind the cards.
  • Net: air enters low and cool, leaves high and hot — the two never short-circuit.

Card placement (why the 6000 is the lower card)

  • PRO 6000 → lower slot (PCI_E2, PCIe 5.0 ×4) so it sits right over the floor + above-PSU intake and drinks fresh air. The ×4 link is a non-issue for a 96 GB card that keeps its job in its own memory.
  • 5090 Astral LC → top slot (PCI_E1, ×8). It's liquid-cooled, so it doesn't need the floor feed — its heat goes to the front radiator.
  • They sit ~4 slot-positions apart, so there's clear air between them. Held by the case's built-in anti-sag GPU bracket — no vertical kit, no riser.

NEVER The one mistake to avoid

Do not put a radiator on the floor or the above-PSU shelf. Those two banks are fresh-air fans only. A radiator there would pre-heat the 6000's intake and cost it 7–9 °C — the difference between "runs cool" and "throttles." Radiators go on the front and top; fresh air goes under the GPU.
